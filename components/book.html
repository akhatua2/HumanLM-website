The picture book Thor: Heroes and Villains is a guide to the citizens of Asgard, its enemies, and the connected realm of Midgard, better known to mortals as planet Earth. Characters depicted include Odin, Frigga, Thor, Loki, the Warriors Three, Lady Sif, Heimdall, the Frost Giants, the Destroyer, Jane Foster, and Philip Coulson. The following information for each character is provided: name, aliases, relatives, abilities, attributes, weapons, and story. Thus, this book serves as an encyclopedia of the characters of the popular 2011 movie Thor. Though a short picture book, I found this volume to be quite useful and informative.

Reviewed in the United States on April 27, 2018
This space saga continues as exciting as ever. Unlike a lot of graphic novels, the violence is not superfluous and formulaic. Art and layout give this series its own unique look. Robots revolting are certainly not something new in science fiction or comics, but this has great characters and story telling. However, I think waiting for more volumes to come out would make a more satisfying read. It has a very fast pace, and this five-issue collection seems like it is made to be read in one or two sittings. I feel like I read one comic book. I admit I read the first four volumes back-to-back. Perhaps there will be a big deluxe edition. Jeff Lemire remains one of comics' most consistently effective writers.
