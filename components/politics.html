The Democratic presidential hopefuls are starting their campaigns already. It may be too little too late if Trump cancels elections, including the 2028 presidential election. But let's pretend for a moment that these are normal times and the Democrats have lost a big election.

The first was California governor Gavin Newsom (D). God, it feels like he has been running for president forever, doesn't it? He's shaking things up, interviewing guests from across the political spectrum including Charlie Kirk and Steve Bannon. This started a firestorm among Democrats, but I think it was a smart move.

Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (D-N.Y.) has been barnstorming through Arizona, Nevada and Colorado on a "Fighting Oligarchy" tour with Sen. Bernie Sanders (I-VT), drawing huge, dare I say Trump-sized, crowds. I've been a huge fan of AOC and the Squad since she was elected.

How the U.S. Became Fascism's Greatest Success Story

Our 'land of the free' is collapsing into an autocratic nightmare

RK
Robert Krulwich
Published in Political Analysis 路 15 min read 路 Jun 2025

It's 2025, and 'the land of the free' is dead and gone. Kaput. The once-inspiring 'shining city on the hill' has vanished. So has the American dream of a wondrous 'land of opportunity.' These American attributes or ideals were never fully realized, sadly, but at least the promise was still there - until recently.

The reason the far-right takeover of the U.S. is fascism's greatest 'success story' is simple: the U.S. is both the world's foremost (former) bastion of democracy and by far the largest nation (population: 347,000,000) to ever succumb to autocratic fascism.

What made the U.S. a prime target for a fascist takeover? In my opinion, our super-individualism and its alter-ego, late-stage capitalism, paved the way and made U.S. fascism almost inevitable. Both systems are based on selfishness, greed, and endless competition.
